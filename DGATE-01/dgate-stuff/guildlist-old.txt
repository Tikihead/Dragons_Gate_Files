2. The Arachnians

The Arachnians are a highly intelligent race of spider-like beings.  They are one of the oldest races in the world and are known to inhabit parts of the Great Waste.  The Great Waste is located on the northern portion of Spur's continent, across the Great Divide.  Legend has it that eons ago these beings fought a mighty war with the Penthanians and paid a bitter price for it.  The war was going very well for the Arachnians until, just before what was intended to be the death blow to their foes, the Penthanian mages unleashed a previously unknown force in the world, UnMagick. This swept through the Arachnians like a gale, forever stripping their race of the ability to use and control runes.  Needless to say, this changed the course of the war and the Penthanians were victorious.  What made their defeat even more painful was that even though they were stripped of the ability to control the runes, they are still able to understand their use on a level that even the mighty Frontacians envy.  This has warped the race to the point that all members of them follow Chaos and wish to bring destruction down on ALL of the other races of the world. However, legend has it that the Arachnians were not the instigators of the war and that prior to their defeat (known as the Great Change), they were actually followers of Order.  Additionally, it is also foretold that there will be a day of reckoning with the Penthanians, and that what was lost will be restored.

Reference Material:  Frontacian Archives, Imperial Council Histories & Minutes, Penthanian Legend, Ancient Lore, Library of Usil Elves

Appearance:  Dark spider-like race, approximately 3 feet high.  They are very strong and massive, and can carry a tremendous amount.

Eyes:  Black.

Temperament:  Mean.  They have an axe to grind on anyone who is handy. They wish to bring destruction to ALL the races of the world.  They are warped and single-mindedly biased against any other race.  They serve Chaos.

Tendencies:  Arachnians are so jealous of any of magick using creatures that they have been known to attack on sight.  They do not have a decent bone in their bodies and have a particularly have a great hatred of Penthanians.  Secians annoy them in an unfathomable manner.  They associate freely with Thugians and Oog-ra.  They cannot, or will not, speak Common.

Background:  Arachnians are one of the oldest races, and inhabit the Great Waste.  Long ago, in a great war for domination against the Penthanians, they were robbed of their ability to use runes.  Regardless of this, they have a great understanding of the makings of magick and it will take some pretty fine magick to get one over on any of them.

Advantages:  Natural armor on body, expertise with the theories behind alchemy, high resistance to magick and quick recognition of it.  They can throw web material to hold an opponent, or bite their pinned opponent with strong fangs.  The material needed to spin webs varies in amount from Arachnian to Arachnian and returns slowly after use.  Highly intelligent. Very strong. Great with their 'hands'.

Use commands:  BITE <target> and WEB <size> AT <target>.

Hindrances:  They cannot use rune-magick.  Cannot speak Common.  Their temperament precludes much interaction with others.

Abilities:  Throw webs.  Fang bites.  Ever-increasing natural body protection.
 
Alignment:  Chaotic

Professions:  Preferred: Fighter
Also:  Thief, Cleric, Barbarian

11. The Frontacians

Appearance:  Tall semi-human race.  Thin & sinewy with a pale and dull complexion.  In the middle of their foreheads and their palms are what look like jewels.

Eyes:  White
Hair:  White

Temperament:  Magick is as necessary as breathing.  Frontacians are aloof, and believe all life is expendable if it will benefit them. They are quick to remind you proudly of the Ages when the Frontacian Empire ruled the world.  All life is expendable if it will benefit them, but they hold themselves above the laws of other cultures and societies.  Indeed, they consider themselves so superior to others that they two sets of standards: one for their own kind, another, stricter set for all others.

Tendencies:  They are quick to anger and look down upon any one or thing which cannot use magick.  They are excessively proud of magickally enchanted weapons, and with good reason.  They disdain those who lie & steal. Subject to fits of madness and fatal illness because of a genetic malady caused by some magickal event in their history, they are dependent upon a special drug used weekly.  Miss one dose, they are unstable, magick goes awry for them and around them, and they are weak and ill.  Miss two doses and they are dead.

Background:  Rulers of the greatest single Empire ever to come to power in the world, the Frontacians are an ancient race.  During the Second and Third Ages when their explorations and conquests became legendary, so did their Magick.  There were great magickal bridges connecting the Empire and the other Isle Kingdoms.  Their home is Sorcerer's Isle in the Northern Sea.  While their Empire fell into disarray and ruin several Ages ago, their Magick continues to be some of the most powerful in all of Dragon's Gate. Their name comes from the Ancient Tongue, meaning "Jeweled Ones".  They have the ability to drain the energy from other peoples, using it to replenish their own natural stores.  Other travelers are very wary of any Frontacian because of this.  [And for good reason!]

Advantages:  Great Magickal Ability.  With experience, will be able to enchant some of the finest magickal weapons available.

Commands of use:  DRAIN ENERGY FROM <target>.

Hindrances:  Unstable body chemistry requires expensive drug maintenance. They are not a strong race in constitution or in muscle power.  Few Frontacians ever study the arts of combat without magick.  Most races of the world consider them untrustworthy because of their ability and tendency to indiscriminately drain energy from others when they need it.

Abilities:  Great Magick, Draining fatigue, and the eventual ability to enchant items (not implemented as of February '98.)

Alignment:  Any

Professions:  Preferred: Rune-Mage
Rare:  Fighter

13. The Secian Race

Note: Secians can be found wherever Dragons are wont to be.

Appearance:  Smallest of all the races living in the world, the Secians are TINY.  The tallest among them might reach 1' 5 inches. Their hair is always blonde, their eyes ruby red. They are highly magickal, colorfully winged little fairy-like beings who are charming to behold.

Eyes:   Ruby
Hair:   Blonde

Temperament: Secians are extremely helpful by nature, in fact, if you should meet one when you have been injured, they will begin to heal you immediately.  They are great friends to Dragon kind -- a friendship bound into the roots of the origin of their race.  They are extremely fond of jewels, diamonds being among their most favorite.  Natural healing empaths, these friendly little beings can be more than a little help In an adventuring party.  Highly magickal.  Almost always happy.

Tendencies:  To members of other races, the unexplainable actions of Secians seem driven.  Secians will always rush to the aid of a Dragon.  The relationship between these two races, even between two who have just met is at once intense and seemingly familiar.  This caring and protective attitude is returned by the Dragons in the same way, because Dragon kind are unexplainably protective to these little ones.  Any wounded being will attract their attention and they will attempt to heal them.  Almost always cheery and fun, Secians are either Rune-Mages or members of the Clerical Orders.  They are ill-suited for anything other than magickal combat.  Even though they always serve Order, they can also be helpful to individuals and tasks which may include individuals other than those who serve Order.  They will not break laws or knowingly injure other sentient beings, and they will not lie.

Background:  They are remnants of the powerful city-state of Secia, changed to this new race at the time of The Great Change.  They are both protected by Dragon kind and protectors of it. No one really knows why this special relationship exists between the smallest of the races and the largest, but there is no doubt that there is a strong binding between them.  They are overly fond of jewels and gemstones.  According to some scholars, this may be because during the Great Change the mutations of their bodies forever lost the embedded jewels. [See accounts of this disaster in histories, archives and library collections.]  They still consider Arachnians part of their 'family' and do not understand the hostility they meet with them.  Secians cannot harm any sentient race.  They will always heal any wounded they encounter.  They do not like items made of metal at all, and will not wear armor.  Secians are so tiny that they can carry very little.

Advantages:  Quick recovery. Their small size makes them harder to hit. Very, VERY light and quick.  Very intelligent and charming.  They can fly with very little effort and the entire race is capable of empathic healing.

Hindrances:  Very weak, Very small, able to carry very little.  Their same empathic ability to heal can also work against them.  If a Secian happens to harm a creature in close (non-magickal) combat, he will feel and experience the pain they caused to the creature.  It has been known for a Secian to die from this empathic backlash.

Abilities:  Flight, empathic healing, great healing ability and quick moving.

Alignment:  Orderly

Professions:  Clerical Orders or Rune-Mage

15.  The Penthanians

Note: The Penthanian race originates in the Great Waste north of the Great Divide (which lies to the north of Spur).  The Penthanians reside primarily along the mountain's base, while the Arachnians hold the Waste's coastline.

Appearance:  Tall human appearing race who have bodies of a soft crystalline substance.  They can be colorful, with the colors running in families.

Eyes:  Black

Temperament: Penthanians are aloof and tend to be somewhat unfeeling. They do not have much compassion or sympathy for others. In their dealings with other races, they can be calculating and at times purposefully deceitful.  There is a long standing hatred of Arachnians among the people of this race.  They are envious of Frontacians, who still have flesh as well as magick jewels.  Magick comes easily to Penthanians.

Tendencies:  They are calculating and plan almost any action with great care.  They will look for the advantage in a situation and manipulate the scene until the advantage is even more in their favor.  They have cool heads.  Once you have earned their trust, most will not ever try to deceive you.  These people are a proud and aloof group.  They have lost their feeling in The Great Change when their bodies underwent the awful mutation caused by the releasing of UnMagick.  Some say they also lost part of the feeling in their hearts and souls at this time.  They will serve Entropy, never dedicating to any representative or deity of Order or Chaos. Most of the time, they serve themselves

Background:  Named in their own tongue, "Living Jewels", the Penthanians were the authors of the UnMagick loosed at the Great Change, and suffered a backlash of effect from it which their Mages had not anticipated.  Their bodies are now crystalline, and they have been robbed of most of their sense of feeling.  Hardened, jealous and self centered, they may have also lost the ability to feel with their hearts. Ancient Legends of some accounts say that they were like this even before The Great Change, though.  Before the Great Change, these people were part of the Frontacian Empire, and were a mighty city-state of Mages. Since that time, they have continued to cultivate their magick.  Lost is their flesh, but their new hardened bodies almost make up for this loss and the jewels which used to be part of their bodies.

Advantages:  High Intelligence and Wisdom.  Built like rocks. [literally!]  Able to withstand pain.  Body provides them great protection.

Hindrances:  The same crystalline construction which gives them added protection also makes recovery not as easy as some peoples.

Abilities:  Crystalline body structure helps protect them more so than those who must still be bound to flesh.

Alignment:  Entropic

Professions:  Preferred:  Rune-Mage
Also:  Thief
